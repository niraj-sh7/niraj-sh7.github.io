Kyrie Irving

Point guard. NBA champion. Greatest ball handler of all time. A tour of his career and path throughout the years.

Early Years and Entry

Born in 1992 in Melbourne and raised in New Jersey, Irving starred at St. Patrick High School before a one year stint at Duke. Cleveland drafted him with the first overall pick in 2011, and he won Rookie of the Year with his elite handles and shot creation.

  • 2011 Drafted 1st overall by the Cleveland Cavaliers.
  • 2012 NBA Rookie of the Year.
  • 2014 FIBA World Cup gold and tournament MVP with Team USA.

NBA Career Highlights

Irving’s achievements include the 2016 championship with Cleveland, multiple All-Star selections, and a reputation for late-game shot-making. He later played in Boston and Brooklyn, then moved to Dallas, where he formed a star backcourt. More of his achievements can be found on nba.com

  • 2011 - 2017 Cleveland Cavaliers.
  • 2017 - 2019 Boston Celtics.
  • 2019 - 2023 Brooklyn Nets.
  • 2023 - Present Dallas Mavericks guard.

Kyrie Trivia

Born in Australia
Kyrie was born in Melbourne in 1992 while his father played professionally there, then grew up in New Jersey. It’s why you sometimes see him listed as Australian-American.
The Game 7 dagger
With 53 seconds left in the 2016 Finals Game 7, he hit the go-ahead three over Stephen Curry. That shot sealed Cleveland’s first NBA title.
Uncle Drew
The viral “Uncle Drew” character started in soda commercials and turned into a full feature film in 2018, boosting Kyrie’s pop-culture profile beyond basketball.
